From Earth to Table

The Making of a Kiln & Hearth Piece

Every step is done by hand in our studio. Here's what happens before a piece reaches your doorstep.

🏔️

1. Clay Preparation

We wedge and condition each batch of stoneware clay by hand to remove air pockets and achieve consistent texture before throwing.

🌀

2. Wheel Throwing

Each form is centered and raised on the wheel. Our potters shape cups, bowls, and plates with practiced hands — no two exactly alike.

🎨

3. Glazing & Decoration

After bisque firing, pieces are dipped, poured, or brushed with our house-mixed glazes. Some receive hand-drawn texture or wax-resist patterns.

🔥

4. Kiln Firing

Final glaze firing reaches Cone 10 — over 2300°F. The extreme heat vitrifies the clay and transforms raw glazes into their finished, food-safe state.
