What the Kiln Teaches You About Patience

You load the kiln on a Thursday. You open it the following Monday. Five days of not knowing. Every potter has a story about a firing that went wrong — and how it changed them.

Setting a Slow Table

There is a particular pleasure in setting a table with things that have weight. Not heavy — weighted. Objects that know what they are and ask nothing of you.

“It's the weight that gets you. You pick it up and immediately understand why cheap ceramics feel hollow.”
“I bought the bud vase for myself. I haven't put anything else in it for six months.”
“The bowl is the first thing I reach for. For everything. It's become part of how I cook.”
“These are objects that make you slow down. I didn't expect that.”
